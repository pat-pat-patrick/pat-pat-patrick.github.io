🧠 Where Does the Money Come From?

Financial markets are filled with people, and people have emotions. My system is a computer program devoid of feelings, designed specifically to profit when "others lose their rationality."

When sudden bad news strikes (like a geopolitical crisis), the public panics and sells at any cost, causing prices to "overshoot." The mathematical model calculates this extreme point. Like a calm and calculated recycler, it buys the asset at a steep discount when others are throwing it away in despair, and sells it back once the market regains its sanity.

📅 Quiet Days vs. Special Days:
  • Quiet Days: Like hardworking ants. Market volatility is low, and the system executes small trades, earning modest profits that steadily compound over time.
  • Panic Days: This is the system's "payday." The higher the volatility and the more panicked the crowd, the larger the profit margins the system captures.

The Truth About the "50% Chance of 10 Losses in a Row"

Rest assured, this does NOT mean we have a 50% chance of losing all our money tomorrow.

Think of it like a weather forecast: If we live in a city for a whole year (experiencing hundreds of trades), there is a 50% probability that, over this long period, we will encounter an extreme "10-day continuous rainstorm" of bad trades.

It is merely a statistical **episode** in a long journey, not a daily threat.
